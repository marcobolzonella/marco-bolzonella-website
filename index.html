About

Currently based in Leuven, Belgium.

I am a PhD student in Statistics with a strong research interest in mathematical statistics, non-Euclidean data and non-standard asymptotic theory. My work combines theoretical analysis with simulation studies, alongside the practical implementation of statistical methods in R and Python.

My path so far has moved from clustering astronomical data from the Gaia mission, to likelihood-based inference for particle physics within the ATLAS collaboration at Nikhef, to my current doctoral research on directional data at KU Leuven.

Doctoral Research

Directional statistics deals with data recorded as angles or orientations rather than ordinary real numbers — directions, bearings, and points on a circle or sphere — which require their own probability models and inference tools rather than standard linear methods. My doctoral research develops semi-parametric modeling for this type of data.
